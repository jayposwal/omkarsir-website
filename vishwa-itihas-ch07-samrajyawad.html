1880 में Africa का केवल 10% हिस्सा यूरोपीय नियंत्रण में था। 1914 में 90%। मात्र 34 सालों में — एक पूरा महाद्वीप बंट गया। यह था New Imperialism — उद्योगपतियों की भूख, राजनेताओं की महत्वाकांक्षा और "Civilizing Mission" के आवरण में ढका हुआ सबसे बड़ा जमीनी डकैती का खेल। Lenin (लेनिन) ने सही कहा था: "Imperialism is the highest stage of Capitalism।"

💡 Imperialism को समझें — एक Rajasthan का उदाहरण

सोचो Rajasthan के एक गाँव में सोने की खदान है। कोई बड़ा शहर का businessman आता है — पहले trader बनकर, फिर moneylender बनकर, फिर धीरे-धीरे जमीन ले लेता है। कहता है — "हम तुम्हें civilized करेंगे, school-hospital देंगे।" लेकिन असल में सोना निकालता है, मजदूरी कम देता है, और अपने देश को भेजता है। यही था Europe का Africa और Asia के साथ। Diamonds Rajasthan से गए — कारोबार London में हुआ। यही Imperialism है।

1अर्थ, परिभाषा एवं अंतर

साम्राज्यवाद (Imperialism)
  • एक शक्तिशाली राज्य द्वारा अन्य राज्यों पर राजनीतिक, आर्थिक और सांस्कृतिक प्रभुत्व
  • Direct या Indirect — दोनों तरह से
  • Formal Control जरूरी नहीं — economic dominance भी
  • Lenin: "Imperialism = Monopoly Capitalism की उच्चतम अवस्था"
  • J.A. Hobson: Surplus capital को overseas invest करने की जरूरत
उपनिवेशवाद (Colonialism)
  • Imperialism का एक specific रूप — DIRECT territorial control
  • Colony में settlers आकर बस जाते हैं
  • कानूनी प्रशासनिक नियंत्रण — political subjugation
  • अपनी भाषा, संस्कृति, कानून थोपना
  • जैसे: British India, French Algeria, Dutch Indonesia

2साम्राज्यवाद के कारण (Causes — Multi-Dimensional)

A. आर्थिक कारण (Economic Causes) — सबसे प्रमुख

1. Raw Material की आवश्यकता: Industrial Revolution के बाद factories को cotton (India), rubber (Congo, Malaya), oil (Middle East), diamonds/gold (South Africa), ivory (Central Africa) चाहिए था

2. नए बाजार (New Markets): Mass production → surplus goods → नए markets की जरूरत। India ही Britain का सबसे बड़ा captive market बना

3. Capital Export (J.A. Hobson का सिद्धांत): Home markets saturated → Surplus capital को overseas invest करना → colonies में Railways, Mines, Plantations

4. सस्ता श्रम (Cheap Labor): Wage differential — Europe की तुलना में colonies में labor 10 गुना सस्ता

5. आर्थिक प्रतिस्पर्धा: France ने Algeria लिया → Britain ने Egypt लिया → Germany ने Cameroon लिया — Competition chain

B. राजनीतिक एवं रणनीतिक कारण (Political & Strategic)

  • Nationalism का उदय: 1871 में Germany और Italy का एकीकरण → नई शक्तियाँ → global prestige की होड़
  • Balance of Power: अगर France ने Africa में colonies लिए तो Britain पीछे क्यों रहे? — Domino effect
  • Strategic Location: Suez Canal (1869) → Egypt अनिवार्य → Britain ने 1882 में control लिया
  • Naval Bases: Coaling Stations की जरूरत — Steam ships को fuel चाहिए → ऐडन, सिंगापुर, जिब्राल्टर
  • Bismarck (बिस्मार्क) का कूटनीतिक खेल: जर्मन को अफ्रीका में उलझाए रखो ताकि आपस में न लड़ें

C. सामाजिक-सांस्कृतिक कारण (Social-Cultural)

  • Social Darwinism: Herbert Spencer (हर्बर्ट स्पेंसर) — "Survival of the Fittest" — Stronger nations should rule weaker
  • "White Man's Burden" (Rudyard Kipling, 1899): गोरे लोगों का "कर्तव्य" है कि एशिया-अफ्रीका को "सभ्य" बनाएं
  • Civilizing Mission: France का "Mission Civilisatrice" — अपनी भाषा, संस्कृति, धर्म थोपना
  • Missionary Activity: Christian missionaries ने भी colonial expansion का रास्ता बनाया — "Bible + Gun"
  • Racial Superiority की भावना: Pseudo-scientific theories — White race श्रेष्ठ है — यही racism की जड़

D. तकनीकी कारण (Technological Causes)

  • Maxim Gun (मैक्सिम गन): 1884 — Hiram Maxim — पहली automatic machine gun — African spears के खिलाफ devastating
  • Steamships और Railways: Deep interior तक पहुँचना संभव — River Steamers ने Congo को खोला
  • Quinine (कुनीन): Malaria के विरुद्ध — अफ्रीका के "Dark Continent" में European survival अब संभव
  • Telegraph: Instant communication — London से दिल्ली तक आदेश — colonial administration आसान

E. वैचारिक कारण (Ideological)

  • J.A. Hobson (जे.ए. हॉब्सन): "Imperialism: A Study" (1902) — Surplus capital theory
  • Lenin (लेनिन): "Imperialism: The Highest Stage of Capitalism" (1917) — Monopoly Capitalism = Imperialism
  • Alfred Thayer Mahan (अल्फ्रेड थायर महान): "Sea Power" theory (1890) — Naval power = World power → colonies जरूरी

4Africa के लिए होड़ — Scramble for Africa

1880 तक Africa का अधिकांश भाग स्वतंत्र था। 1884-85 में Berlin Conference (बर्लिन सम्मेलन) हुआ — 14 यूरोपीय शक्तियाँ बैठीं और बिना किसी African की सहमति के पूरे महाद्वीप को नक्शे पर ruler से काटकर बाँट दिया। "Effective Occupation" का सिद्धांत दिया — जो वहाँ flag लगा दे, वो उसका।

Berlin Conference (बर्लिन सम्मेलन, 1884-85)

  • आयोजक: Otto von Bismarck (ओटो वॉन बिस्मार्क) — German Chancellor
  • 14 European powers + USA — एक भी African representative नहीं
  • "Effective Occupation" Rule: किसी क्षेत्र पर claim करने के लिए physically control होना जरूरी
  • Congo Free State: Belgium के King Leopold II (किंग लियोपोल्ड द्वितीय) को personal property
  • Result: 1914 तक Africa का 90% colonized — केवल Ethiopia और Liberia स्वतंत्र

परीक्षा तथ्य: Berlin Conference (1884-85) में एकमात्र स्वतंत्र African देश Ethiopia (Battle of Adwa 1896 — Italy को हराया) और Liberia (USA द्वारा स्थापित) बचे। Ethiopia = Africa की independence का symbol।

Congo का खूनी अध्याय — King Leopold II

Congo में King Leopold II (किंग लियोपोल्ड द्वितीय) का शासन इतिहास के सबसे brutal examples में से एक है। Rubber production का quota पूरा न करने पर Congolese लोगों के हाथ काट दिए जाते थे। लगभग 1 करोड़ लोग मारे गए। Adam Hochschild (एडम होक्सचाइल्ड) की पुस्तक "King Leopold's Ghost" (1998) ने इसे विश्व के सामने रखा।

Boer War (बोअर युद्ध, 1899-1902) — South Africa

  • Boers: South Africa के Dutch settlers (Afrikaners) — पहले से वहाँ रहते थे
  • Cause: Transvaal में Gold और Diamonds मिले (1886) — Britain ने control चाहा
  • Cecil Rhodes (सेसिल रोड्स): "Cape to Cairo" dream — British imperialist का symbol
  • Concentration Camps: British ने Boer women-children को camps में बंद किया — 25,000+ मृत
  • Result: 1902 — Treaty of Vereeniging — South Africa British colony बनी
  • Indian connection: Mahatma Gandhi (महात्मा गांधी) South Africa में Boer War में Indian Ambulance Corps के साथ — पहला political experience

5Asia में साम्राज्यवाद (Imperialism in Asia)

5.1 China — अफीम युद्ध एवं Spheres of Influence

China 1800 तक विश्व की सबसे बड़ी अर्थव्यवस्था थी। Qing Dynasty (क्विंग राजवंश) का शासन था। Britain China से Tea, Silk, Porcelain खरीदता था पर China को कुछ भी नहीं बेच पाता था। Trade deficit — इसे हल करने के लिए Britain ने India से opium (अफीम) China को बेचना शुरू किया। China ने रोका → Opium Wars।

  • Open Door Policy (1899): USA Secretary of State John Hay (जॉन हे) — सभी powers को China में equal trading rights
  • इसका अर्थ: अमेरिका ने Chinese बाजार को बंद होने से बचाया — अपने interest के लिए
💡 China के Spheres of Influence — Cricket Analogy

सोचो एक cricket ground है जो किसी का नहीं। अलग-अलग teams आईं और बिना ownership के हर team ने अपना corner mark कर लिया — "यह मेरा area है।" China के साथ ऐसा ही हुआ। Russia, France, Germany, Britain, Japan — सबने अपना "sphere" declare किया। China officially independent था पर practically 6 masters के आगे झुक रहा था। USA ने कहा — सबको equal access दो — ताकि वो भी खेल सके।

5.3 India — British Colonial Rule

India British Imperialism का सबसे बड़ा और सबसे महत्वपूर्ण उदाहरण है। East India Company (ईस्ट इंडिया कंपनी, 1600) ने व्यापार से शुरू किया — 1757 के Plassey युद्ध के बाद political power मिली — 1857 के विद्रोह के बाद Crown Rule (ताज का शासन) शुरू हुआ।

► हर साल India से £200 million Britain जाता था — एकतरफा

  • Deindustrialization: Handloom, Textile, Metal industries destroyed — Manchester mills ने India को captive market बनाया
  • Permanent Settlement (1793): Lord Cornwallis (लॉर्ड कॉर्नवालिस) — Zamindari system — peasants exploited
  • Famines: 1876-79, 1896-97, 1943 Bengal Famine — British policies ने अकालों को बढ़ाया
  • Racial discrimination: "Dogs and Indians not allowed" — Clubs में Indians banned

6Japan — एशिया का अपवाद (The Exception)

Japan एकमात्र Asian देश था जो colonized नहीं हुआ बल्कि खुद imperialist बन गया। 1853 में American Commodore Matthew Perry (मैथ्यू पेरी) ने "Black Ships" के साथ Japan को forced trade पर राजी किया। इससे Japan को अहसास हुआ — या modernize करो या colonized हो जाओ।

Meiji Restoration (मेजी पुनर्स्थापना, 1868)

  • Emperor Meiji (सम्राट मेजी): Shogunate (सैन्य शासन) समाप्त — Emperor को शक्ति — "Rich Country, Strong Army" (Fukoku Kyohei)
  • Rapid Westernization: German Constitution model, French army, British navy, American education
  • Industrialization: Silk, Coal, Steel — Government-owned factories → Private hands
  • Sino-Japanese War (1894-95): China को हराया → Taiwan मिला — Asia को चौंकाया
  • Russo-Japanese War (1904-05): Russia को हराया → Korea, Manchuria — एशियाई देश ने यूरोपीय शक्ति को हराया — Asia को प्रेरणा
  • Japan's Imperialism: Korea (1910), Manchuria (1931), China (1937) — "Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere"

परीक्षा महत्व: Russo-Japanese War (1904-05) में Japan की जीत का असर भारत पर: Bal Gangadhar Tilak (तिलक), Lala Lajpat Rai (लाला लाजपत राय) जैसे nationalist leaders ने कहा — "अगर Japan यूरोप को हरा सकता है तो हम भी आजाद हो सकते हैं।" यह Indian Nationalism के लिए turning point था।

8बहुआयामी प्रभाव (Multi-Dimensional Impact)

A. आर्थिक प्रभाव (Economic Impact)

1. Extraction Economy: Plantation system — Rubber (Congo, Malaya), Cotton (India, Egypt), Coffee (Kenya) — केवल export के लिए

2. Deindustrialization: Colonies की cottage industries नष्ट — India का textile, Bengal की silk

3. Monoculture: एक ही फसल → famine risk → 1876 Great Famine

4. Infrastructure for Exploitation: Railways, Roads, Ports — Colony को loot करने के लिए, develop करने के लिए नहीं

5. Capital Drain: Dadabhai Naoroji, R.C. Dutt (आर.सी. दत्त) — India से £200m/year Britain

6. Triangle Trade: Raw Material → Europe → Finished Goods → Colonies → Profits → Europe

B. राजनीतिक प्रभाव (Political Impact)

1. Artificial Borders: Berlin Conference ने ethnic, linguistic, cultural lines ignore करके straight-line borders खींचे — आज भी Africa के conflicts की जड़

2. Divide and Rule: British policy — Hindu-Muslim, Upper caste-Lower caste, Hindu-Sikh विभाजन

3. Colonial Administration: ICS (Indian Civil Service), District Collector system — आज भी जारी

4. Nationalism का जन्म: Imperialism के विरुद्ध national identity → Indian NC, African NC (South Africa), INC

5. League of Nations Mandates (1919): WWI के बाद German-Ottoman colonies को France-Britain को "mandate" — नाम बदला, खेल वही

C. सामाजिक-सांस्कृतिक प्रभाव (Social-Cultural)

1. Racism का संस्थानीकरण: Apartheid (South Africa, 1948-1994), Jim Crow Laws (USA) — colonial racial hierarchy का विस्तार

2. Cultural Suppression: Local languages, customs, religions पर हमला — "Macaulay's Minute" — English-medium education

3. Mission Schools: Literacy बढ़ी — पर Western values थोपी गईं

4. Slavery का अंत: Industrial colonies को free labor चाहिए था → formal slavery abolished → पर exploitative labor जारी

5. Demographic Change: Indentured labor — Indians को Fiji, Mauritius, South Africa, Trinidad ले गए

D. दीर्घकालिक प्रभाव (Long-term Legacy)

  • Neo-colonialism (नव-उपनिवेशवाद): Formal independence के बाद भी economic dependence — Kwame Nkrumah (क्वामे नक्रूमा) का concept
  • Third World Poverty: Colonial extraction → आज भी Africa, Asia, Latin America पिछड़े
  • Refugee Crisis की जड़ें: Artificial borders → ethnic conflicts → Syria, Somalia, Sudan
  • Decolonization (1945-1975): India (1947), Indonesia (1949), Ghana (1957), Algeria (1962), Angola (1975)

💡 Understand Imperialism – An Example from Rajasthan

Imagine there's a gold mine in a village in Rajasthan. A businessman from a big city arrives—first posing as a trader, then as a moneylender, and then gradually acquires the land. He says, "We'll civilize you, provide schools and hospitals." But in reality, he extracts the gold, pays low wages, and sends it back to his country. This is how Europe interacted with Africa and Asia. Diamonds went from Rajasthan—the trade took place in London. This is Imperialism.

1Meaning, Definition and Difference

Imperialism
  • Political, economic and cultural dominance by a powerful state over other states
  • Direct or Indirect – Both Ways
  • Formal control is not necessary – economic dominance is also
  • Lenin: "Imperialism = the highest stage of Monopoly Capitalism"
  • J.A. Hobson: Surplus capital needs to be invested overseas
Colonialism
  • A specific form of Imperialism—DIRECT territorial control
  • Settlers come and settle in the colony
  • Legal administrative control — political subjugation
  • Imposing one's own language, culture, and laws
  • English: British India, French Algeria, Dutch Indonesia

2Causes of Imperialism (Multi-Dimensional)

A. Economic Causes – Most important

1. Raw Material Requirement: After the Industrial Revolution, factories needed cotton (India), rubber (Congo, Malaya), oil (Middle East), diamonds/gold (South Africa), ivory (Central Africa)

2. New Markets: Mass production → surplus goods → need for new markets. India became Britain's largest captive market.

3. Capital Export (J.A. Hobson's theory): Home markets saturated → Surplus capital invested overseas → Railways, Mines, Plantations in colonies

4. Cheap Labor: Wage differential – labor in colonies is 10 times cheaper than in Europe

5. Economic competition: France took Algeria → Britain took Egypt → Germany took Cameroon — Competition chain

B. Political & Strategic

  • Rise of Nationalism: Unification of Germany and Italy in 1871 → New powers → Competition for global prestige
  • Balance of Power: If France acquired colonies in Africa, why did Britain lag behind? - Domino effect
  • Strategic Location: Suez Canal (1869) → Egypt mandatory → Britain took control in 1882
  • Naval Bases: Coaling Stations needed — Steam ships needed fuel → Aden, Singapore, Gibraltar
  • Bismarck's diplomatic game: keep the Germans busy in Africa so they don't fight each other

C. Socio-Cultural

  • Social Darwinism: Herbert Spencer — "Survival of the Fittest" — Stronger nations should rule weaker
  • "White Man's Burden" (Rudyard Kipling, 1899): White people have a "duty" to "civilize" Asia and Africa
  • Civilizing Mission: France's "Mission Civilisatrice"—imposing its language, culture, and religion
  • Missionary Activity: Christian missionaries also paved the way for colonial expansion — "Bible + Gun"
  • Feeling of Racial Superiority: Pseudo-scientific theories—that the White race is superior—are the root of racism.

D. Technological Causes

  • Maxim Gun: 1884 — Hiram Maxim — first automatic machine gun — devastating against African spears
  • Steamships and Railways: Deep Interior Access Possible — River Steamers Open the Congo
  • Quinine: Against Malaria — European survival now possible in Africa's "Dark Continent"
  • Telegraph: Instant communication — orders from London to Delhi — colonial administration easier

E. Ideological

  • J.A. Hobson (J.A. Hobson): "Imperialism: A Study" (1902) — Surplus capital theory
  • Lenin (लेनिन): "Imperialism: The Highest Stage of Capitalism" (1917) — Monopoly Capitalism = Imperialism
  • Alfred Thayer Mahan: "Sea Power" theory (1890) — Naval power = World power → colonies necessary

4Scramble for Africa

By 1880, most of Africa was independent. In 1884-85, the Berlin Conference took place – 14 European powers met and, without the consent of any African, divided the entire continent by drawing a ruler on a map. They proposed the principle of "Effective Occupation" – whoever planted a flag there, it was theirs.

Berlin Conference (Berlin Conference, 1884-85)

  • Organizer: Otto von Bismarck (Otto von Bismarck) — German Chancellor
  • 14 European powers + USA — not a single African representative
  • "Effective Occupation" Rule: Physical control is required to claim an area.
  • Congo Free State: Personal property of King Leopold II of Belgium
  • Result: 90% of Africa colonized by 1914 — only Ethiopia and Liberia independent

Exam Facts:At the Berlin Conference (1884-85), the only independent African countries remaining were Ethiopia (Battle of Adwa 1896—defeated Italy) and Liberia (established by the USA). Ethiopia = a symbol of African independence.

Congo's Bloody Chapter – King Leopold II

The reign of King Leopold II in Congo is one of the most brutal examples in history. Congolese people were subjected to severed hands if they failed to meet rubber production quotas. Approximately 10 million people died. Adam Hochschild's book "King Leopold's Ghost" (1998) brought this to the world's attention.

Boer War (Boer War, 1899-1902) — South Africa

  • Boers: Dutch settlers of South Africa (Afrikaners) — already lived there
  • Cause: Gold and diamonds found in Transvaal (1886) – Britain sought control
  • Cecil Rhodes: "Cape to Cairo" dream — symbol of British imperialist
  • Concentration Camps: British imprisoned Boer women and children in camps—25,000+ dead
  • Result: 1902 — Treaty of Vereeniging — South Africa British colony बनी
  • Indian connection: Mahatma Gandhi with the Indian Ambulance Corps in the Boer War in South Africa – his first political experience

5Imperialism in Asia

5.1 China — Opium Wars and Spheres of Influence

China was the world's largest economy until 1800, under the rule of the Qing Dynasty. Britain bought tea, silk, and porcelain from China, but could not sell anything to China. To address this trade deficit, Britain began selling opium from India to China. China stopped it, leading to the Opium Wars.

💡 China के Spheres of Influence — Cricket Analogy

Imagine a cricket ground that belongs to no one. Different teams came and, without ownership, each team marked its corner—"This is my territory." The same thing happened with China. Russia, France, Germany, Britain, Japan—all declared their own "sphere." China was officially independent but practically bowing to the six masters. The USA said—give everyone equal access—so that they could play too.

5.3 India — British Colonial Rule

India is the largest and most important example of British Imperialism. The East India Company (1600) started with trade – gained political power after the Battle of Plassey in 1757 – Crown Rule began after the Revolt of 1857.

  • Deindustrialization: Handloom, Textile, Metal industries destroyed — Manchester mills ने India को captive market बनाया
  • Permanent Settlement (1793): Lord Cornwallis — Zamindari system — peasants exploited
  • Famines: 1876–79, 1896–97, 1943 Bengal Famine — British policies increased famines
  • Racial discrimination: "Dogs and Indians not allowed" — Clubs में Indians banned

6Japan — The Exception of Asia

Japan was the only Asian country that did not colonize but instead became imperialist. In 1853, American Commodore Matthew Perry forced Japan into trade with the "Black Ships." This made Japan realize—either modernize or be colonized.

Meiji Restoration (Meiji Restoration, 1868)

  • Emperor Meiji: Shogunate (military rule) ended — power to the Emperor — "Rich Country, Strong Army" (Fukoku Kyohei)
  • Rapid Westernization: German Constitution model, French army, British navy, American education
  • Industrialization: Silk, Coal, Steel — Government-owned factories → Private hands
  • Sino-Japanese War (1894-95): China defeated, Taiwan acquired – Asia shocked
  • Russo-Japanese War (1904-05): Russia defeated Korea, Manchuria – Asian country defeated European power – inspiration to Asia
  • Japan's Imperialism: Korea (1910), Manchuria (1931), China (1937) — "Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere"

Exam Importance:Impact of Japan's victory in the Russo-Japanese War (1904-05) on India: Nationalist leaders like Bal Gangadhar Tilak and Lala Lajpat Rai said – "If Japan can defeat Europe, we too can be free." This was a turning point for Indian Nationalism.

8Multi-Dimensional Impact

A. Economic Impact

1. Extraction Economy: Plantation system — Rubber (Congo, Malaya), Cotton (India, Egypt), Coffee (Kenya) — केवल export के लिए

2. Deindustrialization: Colonies' cottage industries destroyed — India's textile, Bengal's silk

3. Monoculture: Single crop → famine risk → 1876 Great Famine

4. Infrastructure for Exploitation: Railways, Roads, Ports — to loot the colony, not to develop it

5. Capital Drain: Dadabhai Naoroji, R.C. Dutt (R.C. Dutt) — £200m/year from India Britain

6. Triangle Trade: Raw Material → Europe → Finished Goods → Colonies → Profits → Europe

B. Political Impact

1. Artificial Borders: The Berlin Conference ignored ethnic, linguistic, and cultural lines and drew straight-line borders—still the root of Africa's conflicts.

2. Divide and Rule: British policy — Hindu-Muslim, Upper caste-Lower caste, Hindu-Sikh विभाजन

3. Colonial Administration: ICS (Indian Civil Service), District Collector system — Continued today

4. Birth of Nationalism: National identity against Imperialism → Indian NC, African NC (South Africa), INC

5. League of Nations Mandates (1919): The French-British "mandate" of German-Ottoman colonies after WWI—name changed, same game

C. Socio-Cultural Influence

1. Institutionalization of Racism: Apartheid (South Africa, 1948–1994), Jim Crow Laws (USA) — expansion of the colonial racial hierarchy

2. Cultural Suppression: Local languages, customs, religions पर हमला — "Macaulay's Minute" — English-medium education

3. Mission Schools: Literacy increased—but Western values ​​were imposed

4. End of slavery: Industrial colonies needed free labor → formal slavery abolished → but exploitative labor continued

5. Demographic Change: Indentured labor — Indians को Fiji, Mauritius, South Africa, Trinidad ले गए

D. Long-term Legacy

  • Neo-colonialism: Economic dependence despite formal independence – a concept by Kwame Nkrumah
  • Third World Poverty: Colonial extraction → Even today Africa, Asia, Latin America are backward
  • Roots of the Refugee Crisis: Artificial borders → ethnic conflicts → Syria, Somalia, Sudan
  • Decolonization (1945-1975): India (1947), Indonesia (1949), Ghana (1957), Algeria (1962), Angola (1975)

9India and Rajasthan Connection

A. Rajasthan and British Imperialism

  • Subsidiary Alliance: Lord Wellesley 1798 — Rajput states placed their armies under British control

► Jaipur (1803), Jodhpur (1818), Udaipur (1818), Bikaner (1818) — all Rajputana states

  • Rajputana की autonomy: Rajput rulers technically independent — पर practically British control
  • Political Agents: British Resident agents in every state — real power
  • Economic Impact: Rajasthan के textiles — Jaipur की printed fabrics, Jodhpur की leather — British competition से hurt
  • Salt Tax: British monopoly on Rajasthan's salt lakes (Sambhar, Pachpadra, Lunkaransar)
  • Opium: poppy cultivation in Rajasthan — opium export to China — in Mewar, Kota

B. Rajasthan's contribution in WWI

  • Rajputana Rifles: "loyal soldiers" of British imperialism — fought in Palestine, Mesopotamia, France
  • Sir Ganga Singh of Bikaner: Attended the Paris Peace Conference (1919)—a native ruler from India
  • Jodhpur Lancers: Haifa Battle (1918) — decisive victory in Palestine — still celebrate today

C. Praja Mandal Movement - Resistance to Colonial rule

  • Praja Mandal in the princely states of Rajasthan: Jaipur (1931), Jodhpur (1930s), Mewar Praja Mandal — demand for responsible government
  • All India States People's Conference: Jawaharlal Nehru's presidency — Voice of Indian nationalism in the princely states of Rajasthan
