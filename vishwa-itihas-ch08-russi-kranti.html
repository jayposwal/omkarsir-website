1917 — Russia में एक ऐसा तूफान आया जिसने 300 साल पुरानी Romanov (रोमानोव) सत्ता को महज 8 दिनों में मिटा दिया। और फिर 8 महीने बाद दूसरा तूफान — इस बार Bolsheviks (बोल्शेविक) ने सत्ता ली और दुनिया का पहला Communist राज्य बना। E.H. Carr (ई.एच. कार) ने कहा: "The Russian Revolution is one of the great turning points in modern history." यह सिर्फ Russia की नहीं, पूरी 20वीं सदी की कहानी बदलने वाली घटना थी।

💡 रूसी क्रांति को समझें — Rajasthan की भाषा में

सोचो Rajasthan के एक गाँव में Zamindar (जार = ज़मींदार) है जो 300 साल से राज कर रहा है। 98% किसान गरीब हैं, जमीन नहीं है, कर्ज है, पर बोलने की आज़ादी नहीं। पड़ोसी गाँव (Japan जैसे) से हार होती है — ज़मींदार की इज्जत जाती है। WWI जैसे एक बड़े झगड़े में गाँव के जवान मारे जाते हैं, रोटी नहीं मिलती। एक दिन औरतें सड़क पर निकलीं — "रोटी दो!" — फिर सैनिक भी जुड़े — ज़मींदार ने घर छोड़ा। यही था February Revolution। फिर एक organized group (Bolsheviks) ने नारा दिया — "शांति, जमीन, रोटी" — और सत्ता ले ली। यही था October Revolution।

1रूसी क्रांति — प्रकृति एवं चरण (Nature & Stages)

रूसी क्रांति (1917) 20वीं सदी की सर्वाधिक महत्वपूर्ण घटनाओं में से एक थी। इसने Tsarist autocracy (ज़ारशाही) का अंत किया और एक revolutionary socialist regime की स्थापना की। इतिहासकार इसे तीन चरणों में देखते हैं:

चरणकालमुख्य घटनापरिणाम
1. Revolution of 190522 Jan 1905Bloody Sunday — Gapon marchTsar ने Duma बनाई — October Manifesto
2. February/March Revolution7-15 March 1917Workers Strike, Soldiers join, Tsar abdicateRomanov Dynasty का अंत — Provisional Govt
3. October/November Revolution7 Nov 1917Bolsheviks ने Provisional Govt उखाड़ीविश्व का पहला Communist State

Textbook Revolution: Bolshevik Revolution को "Textbook Revolution" कहा जाता है क्योंकि यह well-defined planning और programs के अनुसार organized तरीके से की गई थी। यह spontaneous नहीं, calculated थी।

21905 की क्रांति — पहला विस्फोट

1905 की क्रांति रूसी क्रांति की पहली रिहर्सल थी। Leon Trotsky (लियोन ट्रॉट्स्की) ने इसे "dress rehearsal for 1917" कहा। यह spontaneous uprising था — planned नहीं।

  • Russo-Japanese War हार (1904-05): Russia ने Japan से हार — सेना की असली हालत उजागर — "Tsar अजेय है" का मिथक टूटा
  • Bloody Sunday (22 January 1905): Father Georgy Gapon (जॉर्ज गापोन) के नेतृत्व में ~1.5 लाख workers St. Petersburg में शांतिपूर्ण मार्च

► मांग: बेहतर काम की शर्तें, political rights, industrial reforms — Petition to Tsar

► Tsarist soldiers ने निहत्थे protesters पर गोली चलाई — सैकड़ों मारे गए

► Result: Russia-wide strikes, peasant revolts, sailors का विद्रोह (Battleship Potemkin)

  • October Manifesto (30 October 1905): Nicholas II (निकोलस द्वितीय) ने घोषणापत्र जारी किया

► Civil rights: Freedom of speech, expression, associations

► State Duma (1906): Representative legislative body का वादा

► लेकिन Tsar ने Duma की शक्तियाँ धीरे-धीरे घटाईं और कई बार भंग किया

3दीर्घकालिक कारण — राजनीतिक (Political Causes)

A. Tsarist Autocracy — निरंकुश शासन

  • Nicholas II (निकोलस द्वितीय, 1894-1917): कमजोर, अयोग्य शासक

► "He lacked the ability to understand political changes and public demands" — Connect Civils

► Repressive measures — political opposition को दबाया — Siberia में निर्वासन

  • Tsarina Alexandra (ज़ारिना एलेक्जेंड्रा) का प्रभाव: Gregory Rasputin (रासपुटिन) पर आश्रित — "Mad Monk"

► Rasputin की policy पर पकड़ — ministers बदलते रहे — प्रशासनिक अराजकता

  • Russification Policy (रूसीकरण नीति): Alexander I से — अल्पसंख्यकों पर Russian भाषा, culture, authority थोपना

► Jews, Poles, Finns, Uzbeks, Tartars, Cossacks, Armenians — सब नाराज

  • कोई effective Parliament नहीं: Duma को बार-बार भंग किया — लोकतांत्रिक मांगें अनसुनी

4दीर्घकालिक कारण — आर्थिक एवं सामाजिक

A. किसानों की दयनीय स्थिति

  • Serfdom (दासता): Alexander II (अलेक्जेंडर द्वितीय) ने 1861 में समाप्त — पर economic condition में सुधार नहीं
  • Land Inequality: 20,000 बड़े जमींदारों के पास 18 करोड़ एकड़ भूमि, 1 करोड़ किसानों के पास 19 करोड़ "rai" (एकड़) — Harkov, Poltava, Volga क्षेत्र में किसान विद्रोह
  • Redemption Payments: 1861 की मुक्ति के बाद किसानों को जमीन के लिए 49 साल तक installments देनी थीं
  • Mir System: सामूहिक ग्राम व्यवस्था — individual progress रोकती थी

B. श्रमिकों की दशा

  • Limited Industrialization: Russia में industrial development uneven — "islands in a vast sea of agriculture" (J.M. Roberts)
  • Terrible Working Conditions: कम वेतन, लंबे घंटे, unsafe workplaces, कोई labor rights नहीं
  • Concentrated Workers: Industries कुछ ही शहरों में — St. Petersburg, Moscow — workers की संगठित शक्ति बनी

► J.M. Roberts: "The gathering of such a large number of workers in one place gave them the opportunity to develop their organizational strength."

C. सामाजिक असमानता

  • Upper Class: Aristocrats, Nobles, Wealthy landowners — सब rights, no responsibilities
  • Lower Class: Peasants + Industrial workers — extreme poverty, no rights
  • Middle Class (Bourgeoisie): Liberal reforms चाहते थे — Kadets, Zemstvo institutions
  • Zemstvo: Local self-government bodies — 1864 में बनाए — पर limited power

5वैचारिक कारण — विचारक एवं दर्शन

A. Socialist Thinkers का प्रभाव

  • Karl Marx (कार्ल मार्क्स): Communist Manifesto (1848) + Das Kapital (1867) — Class Struggle, Dialectical Materialism
  • Maxim Gorky (मैक्सिम गोर्की): Russian novelist — "Mother" (1906) — workers की जिंदगी — political awareness
  • Mikhail Bakunin (मिखाइल बाकुनिन): Anarchism — सभी authority नष्ट करो
  • H.A.L. Fisher: "This socialist propaganda created deep dissatisfaction and hatred among the workers of the country towards Tsarism."

B. साहित्यकारों का योगदान

  • Leo Tolstoy (लियो टॉल्स्टॉय): "War and Peace", "Anna Karenina" — सामाजिक अन्याय की आलोचना
  • Ivan Turgenev (इवान तुर्गेनेव): "Fathers and Sons" — Nihilism की अवधारणा
  • Fyodor Dostoevsky (फ्योदर दोस्तोयेव्स्की): "Crime and Punishment" — नैतिक प्रश्न उठाए
  • इन साहित्यकारों ने readers में राजनीतिक चेतना जगाई

C. Nihilism एवं Populism

  • Nihilism (निहिलिज्म): पारंपरिक institutions, धार्मिक authority और मौजूदा राजनीतिक व्यवस्था को अस्वीकार करना

► लक्ष्य: पुरानी autocratic system को नष्ट करो, नई social order बनाओ

  • Populism (Narodnik Movement): 1870s में — "V narod!" ("जनता के पास जाओ") — intellectuals ने किसानों को जागृत किया

6तात्कालिक कारण — प्रथम विश्व युद्ध (WWI) का प्रभाव

David Thomson (डेविड थॉमसन) ने लिखा: "The Russian Revolution was a direct outcome of the war of 1914-1918." WWI ने Russia के सभी internal contradictions को एक साथ विस्फोट करने पर मजबूर किया।

  • Russia ने WWI में entry (August 1914): State Duma ने सरकार का समर्थन किया — शुरू में उत्साह
  • Heavy Military Defeats: Germany के खिलाफ भारी नुकसान — Tannenberg (1914), Masurian Lakes — लाखों सैनिक मारे
  • Tsar Nicholas II personally commands army (1915): हार की जिम्मेदारी सीधे Tsar पर — legitimacy गई
  • Economic Collapse: War expenditure → inflation, basic goods का अभाव, Black marketing, hoarding

► सरकार ने अधिक currency print की → inflation और बढ़ी

  • Scorched Earth Policy: अपनी ही crops, villages जलाए — दुश्मन के लिए कुछ न छोड़ा — पर civilians को भी नुकसान
  • Food Shortage: Petrograd में रोटी नहीं — यही February Revolution का trigger
💡 WWI का Russia पर असर — Cricket Analogy

सोचो Rajasthan की team एक बड़े tournament में खेल रही है। Team हार रही है, captain (Tsar) ने खुद coaching ले ली — और और हारे। खिलाड़ियों का morale टूट गया, घर में family भूखी है, पैसा नहीं। एक दिन खिलाड़ियों ने कहा — "हम नहीं खेलेंगे, पहले घर जाएंगे।" यही था February 1917 में Russia में — soldiers ने officers का order मानना बंद किया और Revolution join किया।

February Revolution के परिणाम

  • Romanov Dynasty (रोमानोव वंश) का अंत: 300+ वर्षों का शासन समाप्त
  • Petrograd Soviet का गठन: Workers और Soldiers की councils — "Soviet Order No. 1" — सैनिकों को officers नहीं, Soviets की सुनो
  • Provisional Government (अनंतिम सरकार): State Duma members — liberalnoblemen, middle class — Prince Lvov (प्रिंस ल्वोव) अध्यक्ष
  • Reforms announced: Political prisoners मुक्त, exiled revolutionaries वापस, Freedom of speech/press/associations, Greek Church के privileges समाप्त

8Provisional Govt vs Petrograd Soviet — Dual Power (दोहरी सत्ता)

Provisional Government (अनंतिम सरकार)
  • Kadets + moderate socialists
  • War जारी रखना चाहते थे — Allies के साथ
  • Constitutional Assembly तक decisions
  • Milyukov (विदेश मंत्री) — annexationist policy
  • Middle class और elites का प्रतिनिधित्व
  • जनता की मूल मांगें पूरी नहीं कीं
Petrograd Soviet (पेत्रोग्राद सोवियत)
  • Workers + Soldiers representatives
  • तत्काल शांति चाहते थे — "No annexation, no indemnity"
  • जमीन किसानों को, factories workers को
  • Soviet Order No. 1 — military authority
  • Bolsheviks का बढ़ता influence
  • "Peace, Land, Bread" — जनता की आवाज

Lenin का April Theses (अप्रैल थीसिस, April 1917)

Lenin (लेनिन) Switzerland से वापस आए (German-arranged "Sealed Train" से) और April Theses प्रस्तुत किए — Bolsheviks की रणनीति का roadmap।

  • "कोई parliamentary republic नहीं — Soviets की government"
  • "युद्ध तुरंत बंद करो"
  • "Land nationalize करो — peasants को दो"
  • "Banks nationalize करो"
  • "No support to Provisional Government" — बिल्कुल नहीं

July Days और Kerensky Government

  • July Uprising (3 July 1917): Bolsheviks के नेतृत्व में armed demonstrations — Provisional Govt ने army से दबाया
  • Lenin और Zinoviev (ज़िनोवियेव) Finland भागे
  • Alexander Kerensky (अलेक्जेंडर केरेंस्की): नया PM — युद्ध जारी रखा — Galicia में हार — support खोया
  • Kornilov Affair (August 1917): General Kornilov (कोर्निलोव) का coup attempt — Kerensky ने Bolsheviks की मदद ली — Bolsheviks को legitimacy मिली

9October/November Revolution — Bolshevik विजय (7 November 1917)

"Peace, Land, Bread" (शांति, जमीन, रोटी) — यह तीन शब्दों का नारा Bolsheviks की सबसे बड़ी ताकत था। September 1917 तक Bolsheviks ने Petrograd और Moscow Soviets का majority हासिल कर लिया।

Revolution की तैयारी

  • Leon Trotsky (लियोन ट्रॉट्स्की): Military Revolutionary Committee (MRC) का गठन — coup की planning
  • Lenin का आग्रह: "तुरंत action लो — देरी = मौत"
  • Bolsheviks को Petrograd Soviet का majority समर्थन

7 November 1917 — Revolution Night

1. Bolsheviks ने strategic points पर कब्जा: Telephone exchanges, Post offices, Railway stations, Banks, Bridges

2. Aurora Cruiser (Aurora युद्धपोत): रात में Winter Palace पर तोप से signal — Kerensky सरकार को surrender का ultimatum

3. Workers, Soldiers, Sailors ने Provisional Government को उखाड़ फेंका

4. Kerensky (केरेंस्की) राजधानी छोड़कर भागे — कभी वापस नहीं आए

5. Council of People's Commissars (Sovnarkom): Lenin अध्यक्ष — नई सरकार

परीक्षा तथ्य: February Revolution (15 March 1917) = spontaneous, unplanned, junta-led। October Revolution (7 November 1917) = planned, Bolshevik-organized "Textbook Revolution"। Russia का calendar Old Style (Julian) था — इसीलिए February Revolution = March और October Revolution = November।

10Bolshevik सरकार की नीतियाँ (Lenin's Programme)

तत्काल नीतियाँ (Immediate Decrees)

  • Decree on Peace: तत्काल युद्धविराम — Germany से peace talks
  • Decree on Land: Private land का nationalization — मुआवजा नहीं — Peasants को वितरण
  • Decree on Workers Control: Factories पर Workers committees का control
  • Banks का nationalization: Central bank + private banks सब state के
  • Laissez-faire का अंत: State control over production
  • Capitalists के political rights समाप्त
  • Constituent Assembly (November 1917): Elections हुए — SR majority — Bolsheviks ने January 1918 में dissolve किया

12गृह युद्ध एवं Red Terror (1918-1921)

Red vs White War

Red Army (लाल सेना)
  • Bolsheviks — Leon Trotsky (ट्रॉट्स्की) ने बनाई
  • Workers + Peasants
  • Centralized command, clear ideology
  • Lenin के "Red Terror" — opponents का systematic खात्मा
  • Cheka (चेका): Secret Police — Felix Dzerzhinsky
  • Nicholas II + family को July 1918 में Yekaterinburg में गोली मारी
White Army (श्वेत सेना)
  • Anti-Bolshevik forces — royalists, moderate socialists, nationalists
  • Britain, France, USA, Japan का समर्थन (Intervention)
  • Disunited — कोई common ideology नहीं
  • General Denikin, Kolchak, Yudenich — अलग-अलग fronts
  • Peasants का समर्थन नहीं — जमीन वापस लेना चाहते थे
  • 1921 में Red Army की जीत — Civil War समाप्त
  • Civil War (1918-21) में 70 लाख से अधिक मारे गए — युद्ध + famine + disease
  • Cheka (चेका): Felix Dzerzhinsky (फेलिक्स दजेर्जिंस्की) — Secret Police — "Red Terror" — opponents को liquidate
  • War Communism: 1918-21 — सब कुछ state को — forcible grain requisitioning — peasants नाराज

13NEP एवं Stalin का उदय (Post-Revolution Developments)

New Economic Policy — NEP (1921-1928)

Civil War की बर्बादी और 1921 के Kronstadt Rebellion (Kronstadt विद्रोह — Sailors ने ही Bolsheviks के खिलाफ उठे) के बाद Lenin ने tactical retreat की और NEP लागू किया।

  • Small private businesses की अनुमति — पर "Commanding Heights" (banks, industry, transport) state के
  • Peasants को surplus grain market में बेचने की छूट
  • Foreign capital को limited invitation
  • Lenin: NEP = "One step backward, two steps forward"
  • Result: अर्थव्यवस्था recover हुई — 1928 तक pre-war levels

Lenin की मृत्यु और Stalin का उदय (1924-1928)

  • Lenin (लेनिन): 21 January 1924 को मृत्यु — power struggle शुरू
  • Stalin (स्टालिन) vs Trotsky (ट्रॉट्स्की): Stalin ने party machinery पर control — Trotsky निष्कासित (1927), Mexico में हत्या (1940)
  • Joseph Stalin (जोसेफ स्टालिन): General Secretary → अंतिम dictator

► "Socialism in One Country" — पहले Russia को मजबूत करो, फिर world revolution

Stalin's Five Year Plans (पंचवर्षीय योजनाएं, 1928-)

  • First Five Year Plan (1928-32): Rapid industrialization — Steel, Coal, Electricity — Magnitogorsk जैसे cities
  • Collectivization (सामूहिकीकरण): Individual farms → Collective farms (Kolkhoz) — 1929-33

► Kulaks (कुलक = rich peasants) का liquidation — "Dekulakization"

► Ukraine Famine (Holodomor 1932-33): 30-50 लाख यूक्रेनी मारे — Stalin की policy

  • Great Purge (1936-38): Political rivals, military generals, party members — show trials — Gulags
  • USSR 1941 तक दुनिया का दूसरा industrial power बन गया

1917 — A storm swept through Russia, sweeping away the 300-year-old Romanov dynasty in just eight days. And then another storm eight months later — this time the Bolsheviks took power, creating the world's first Communist state. E.H. Carr said: "The Russian Revolution is one of the great turning points in modern history." This was a turning point not just for Russia, but for the entire 20th century.

💡 Understand the Russian Revolution – in the language of Rajasthan

Imagine, in a village in Rajasthan, there is a Zamindar (Tsar = Landlord) who has been ruling for 300 years. 98% of the farmers are poor, they don't have land, they are in debt, but they don't have freedom of speech. There is a defeat in a neighboring village (like Japan) - the Zamindar loses his respect. In a big conflict like WWI, the village's soldiers are killed, but they don't get bread. One day, the women came out on the streets - "Give us bread!" - then the soldiers also joined in - the Zamindar left his house. This was the February Revolution. Then an organized group (Bolsheviks) gave the slogan - "Peace, Land, Bread" - and took power. This was the October Revolution.

1Russian Revolution – Nature and Stages

The Russian Revolution (1917) was one of the most important events of the 20th century. It ended the Tsarist autocracy and established a revolutionary socialist regime. Historians view it in three phases:

phaseperiodMain eventResults
1. Revolution of 190522 Jan 1905Bloody Sunday — Gapon marchTsar creates Duma — October Manifesto
2. February/March Revolution7-15 March 1917Workers Strike, Soldiers join, Tsar abdicateEnd of the Romanov Dynasty — Provisional Govt
3. October/November Revolution7 Nov 1917The Bolsheviks overthrew the Provisional GovernmentThe world's first Communist State

Textbook Revolution: The Bolshevik Revolution is called a "textbook revolution" because it was organized and carried out according to well-defined planning and programs. It was calculated, not spontaneous.

2The Revolution of 1905—The First Outburst

The 1905 Revolution was the first rehearsal for the Russian Revolution. Leon Trotsky called it a "dress rehearsal for 1917." It was a spontaneous uprising—not a planned one.

  • Russo-Japanese War defeat (1904-05): Russia loses to Japan – the true state of the army is exposed – the myth of the "Tsar is invincible" is shattered
  • Bloody Sunday (22 January 1905): Led by Father Georgy Gapon, approximately 150,000 workers march peacefully through St. Petersburg.

► Demands: Better working conditions, political rights, industrial reforms — Petition to Tsar

► Tsarist soldiers fired on unarmed protesters—hundreds were killed

► Result: Russia-wide strikes, peasant revolts, sailors' revolt (Battleship Potemkin)

  • October Manifesto (30 October 1905): Nicholas II (Nicholas II) issued a manifesto

► Civil rights: Freedom of speech, expression, associations

► State Duma (1906): Promise of a Representative Legislative Body

► But the Tsar gradually reduced the Duma's powers and dissolved it several times.

4Long-term causes – economic and social

A. The miserable condition of the farmers

  • Serfdom: abolished by Alexander II in 1861—but economic conditions did not improve
  • Land Inequality: 20,000 large landowners owned 180 million acres of land, while 10 million peasants owned 190 million "rai" (acres) — Peasant Revolt in Harkov, Poltava, Volga Region
  • Redemption Payments: After the liberation of 1861, farmers had to pay installments for their land for 49 years.
  • Mir System: Collective village system – prevented individual progress

B. Condition of workers

  • Limited Industrialization: Russia में industrial development uneven — "islands in a vast sea of agriculture" (J.M. Roberts)
  • Terrible Working Conditions: Low pay, long hours, unsafe workplaces, no labor rights
  • Concentrated Workers: Industries In only a few cities—St. Petersburg, Moscow—workers became organized forces.

► J.M. Roberts: "The gathering of such a large number of workers in one place gave them the opportunity to develop their organizational strength."

C. Social inequality

  • Upper Class: Aristocrats, Nobles, Wealthy landowners — सब rights, no responsibilities
  • Lower Class: Peasants + Industrial workers — extreme poverty, no rights
  • Middle Class (Bourgeoisie): wanted liberal reforms — Kadets, Zemstvo institutions
  • Zemstvo: Local self-government bodies—created in 1864—but limited power

5Ideological reasons – thinkers and philosophy

A. Influence of Socialist Thinkers

  • Karl Marx: Communist Manifesto (1848) + Das Kapital (1867) — Class Struggle, Dialectical Materialism
  • Maxim Gorky: Russian novelist — "Mother" (1906) — workers' lives — political awareness
  • Mikhail Bakunin: Anarchism—destroy all authority
  • H.A.L. Fisher: "This socialist propaganda created deep dissatisfaction and hatred among the workers of the country towards Tsarism."

B. Contribution of writers

  • Leo Tolstoy: "War and Peace", "Anna Karenina" — criticism of social injustice
  • Ivan Turgenev: "Fathers and Sons" — The Concept of Nihilism
  • Fyodor Dostoevsky: "Crime and Punishment" — raises moral questions
  • These writers awakened political consciousness among the readers.

C. Nihilism and Populism

  • Nihilism: Rejection of traditional institutions, religious authority, and the existing political system

► Goal: Destroy the old autocratic system and create a new social order

  • Populism (Narodnik Movement): In the 1870s—"V narod!" ("Go to the people")—intellectuals awakened the peasants

6Immediate Cause – Impact of World War I (WWI)

David Thomson wrote: "The Russian Revolution was a direct outcome of the war of 1914–1918." WWI forced all of Russia's internal contradictions to explode at once.

  • Russia enters WWI (August 1914): The State Duma supports the government—initially enthusiastic
  • Heavy Military Defeats: Heavy losses against Germany – Tannenberg (1914), Masurian Lakes – millions of soldiers killed
  • Tsar Nicholas II personally commands the army (1915): The responsibility for the defeat fell directly on the Tsar — ​​legitimacy
  • Economic Collapse: War expenditure → inflation, basic goods का अभाव, Black marketing, hoarding

► The government printed more currency → inflation increased further

  • Scorched Earth Policy: Burn your own crops and villages—leaving nothing for the enemy—but harming civilians too
  • Food Shortage: No bread in Petrograd—this is the trigger of the February Revolution
💡 The impact of WWI on Russia — Cricket Analogy

Imagine the Rajasthan team playing in a major tournament. The team was losing, the captain (the Tsar) himself took over coaching—and they lost even more. The players' morale was broken, their families were hungry and had no money. One day, the players declared, "We won't play. We'll go home first." This is what happened in Russia in February 1917—soldiers stopped obeying their officers' orders and joined the Revolution.

8Provisional Government vs. Petrograd Soviet — Dual Power

Provisional Government
  • Cadets + moderate socialists
  • War wanted to continue—with Allies
  • Constitutional Assembly तक decisions
  • Milyukov (Foreign Minister) — annexationist policy
  • Representation of the middle class and elites
  • The basic demands of the people were not met.
Petrograd Soviet
  • Workers + Soldiers representatives
  • Wanted immediate peace – "No annexation, no indemnity"
  • Land to farmers, factories to workers
  • Soviet Order No. 1 — military authority
  • Growing influence of the Bolsheviks
  • "Peace, Land, Bread" — Voice of the People

Lenin's April Theses (April 1917)

Lenin returned from Switzerland (from the German-arranged "Sealed Train") and presented the April Theses—a roadmap of the Bolsheviks' strategy.

  • "No parliamentary republic—a government of Soviets"
  • "Stop the war immediately"
  • "Nationalize the land – give it to the peasants"
  • "Nationalize the banks"
  • "No support to Provisional Government" — absolutely not

July Days और Kerensky Government

  • July Uprising (3 July 1917): Bolshevik-led armed demonstrations—Provisional Government suppressed by army
  • Lenin and Zinoviev fled to Finland
  • Alexander Kerensky: New PM — continued the war — lost Galicia — lost support
  • Kornilov Affair (August 1917): Coup attempt by General Kornilov — Kerensky seeks Bolshevik support — Bolsheviks gain legitimacy

9October/November Revolution — Bolshevik विजय (7 November 1917)

"Peace, Land, Bread"—this three-word slogan was the Bolsheviks' greatest strength. By September 1917, the Bolsheviks had gained a majority in the Petrograd and Moscow Soviets.

Preparation for the Revolution

  • Leon Trotsky: Formation of the Military Revolutionary Committee (MRC) – planning of the coup
  • Lenin urged: "Take immediate action—delay = death."
  • The Bolsheviks received the support of the majority of the Petrograd Soviet.

7 November 1917 — Revolution Night

1. Bolsheviks ने strategic points पर कब्जा: Telephone exchanges, Post offices, Railway stations, Banks, Bridges

2. Aurora Cruiser: Cannon signal at night on the Winter Palace—ultimatum of surrender to the Kerensky government

3. Workers, soldiers, and sailors overthrew the Provisional Government.

4. Kerensky fled the capital—never to return

5. Council of People's Commissars (Sovnarkom): Lenin chairman — new government

Exam Facts:February Revolution (15 March 1917) = spontaneous, unplanned, junta-led। October Revolution (7 November 1917) = planned, Bolshevik-organized "Textbook Revolution"। Russia का calendar Old Style (Julian) था — इसीलिए February Revolution = March और October Revolution = November।

Immediate Decrees

  • Decree on Peace: Immediate ceasefire — peace talks with Germany
  • Decree on Land: Nationalization of private land—no compensation—distribution to peasants
  • Decree on Workers Control: Factories पर Workers committees का control
  • Banks का nationalization: Central bank + private banks सब state के
  • Laissez-faire (का अंत): State control over production
  • Political rights of capitalists ended
  • Constituent Assembly (November 1917): Elections held — SR majority — dissolved by Bolsheviks in January 1918

11Treaty of Brest-Litovsk (Treaty of Brest-Litovsk, 3 March 1918)

Lenin accepted the "obscene peace" – accepted all the conditions of Germany because he thought: first stabilize the revolution in Russia, then there will be a revolution in Germany too.

Importance: The Treaty of Brest-Litovsk (1918) kept Russia out of WWI. But after Germany's defeat in WWI (Nov 1918), the treaty became void—Russia regained lost territories.

12Civil War and Red Terror (1918–1921)

Red vs White War

Red Army
  • Bolsheviks – formed by Leon Trotsky
  • Workers + Peasants
  • Centralized command, clear ideology
  • Lenin's "Red Terror"—the systematic elimination of opponents
  • Cheka (चेका): Secret Police — Felix Dzerzhinsky
  • Nicholas II and his family were shot in Yekaterinburg in July 1918.
White Army
  • Anti-Bolshevik forces — royalists, moderate socialists, nationalists
  • Support from Britain, France, USA, Japan (Intervention)
  • Disunited — no common ideology
  • General Denikin, Kolchak, Yudenich — अलग-अलग fronts
  • Peasants did not support – wanted to take back the land
  • Red Army victory in 1921 – Civil War ends
  • Over 7 million died in the Civil War (1918-21) – war + famine + disease
  • Cheka: Felix Dzerzhinsky — Secret Police — "Red Terror" — liquidate opponents
  • War Communism: 1918-21 — Everything to the state — Forcible grain requisitioning — Peasants angry

13NEP and the rise of Stalin (Post-Revolution Developments)

New Economic Policy — NEP (1921-1928)

After the devastation of the Civil War and the Kronstadt Rebellion of 1921 (the sailors themselves rose up against the Bolsheviks), Lenin made a tactical retreat and implemented the NEP.

  • Small private businesses allowed — on "Commanding Heights" (banks, industry, transport) of the state
  • Peasants are allowed to sell their surplus grains in the market.
  • Foreign capital को limited invitation
  • Lenin: NEP = "One step backward, two steps forward"
  • Result: The economy recovered—to pre-war levels by 1928

Lenin's death and Stalin's rise (1924–1928)

  • Lenin: Death on 21 January 1924 — power struggle begins
  • Stalin vs Trotsky: Stalin controlled the party machinery — Trotsky expelled (1927), assassinated in Mexico (1940)
  • Joseph Stalin: General Secretary → Last dictator

► "Socialism in One Country" — First strengthen Russia, then world revolution

Stalin's Five Year Plans (1928-)

  • First Five Year Plan (1928-32): Rapid industrialization — Steel, Coal, Electricity — Magnitogorsk जैसे cities
  • Collectivization: Individual farms → Collective farms (Kolkhoz) — 1929-33

► Liquidation of the Kulaks (Kulak = rich peasants) — "Dekulakization"

► Ukraine Famine (Holodomor 1932-33): 3-5 million Ukrainians killed – Stalin's policy

  • Great Purge (1936-38): Political rivals, military generals, party members — show trials — Gulags
  • The USSR became the world's second industrial power by 1941.

14Multi-Dimensional Impact of the Russian Revolution

A. Political influence

1. The world's first Communist State: a practical test of Marxist ideas — "laboratory of Marxism"

2. Rise of USSR as a World Power: Super Power with USA after 1945

3. Inspiration to the Third World: Anti-imperialist movements in Asia and Africa — China (1949), Cuba (1959), Vietnam, Korea

4. Civil Liberties का दमन: One-party state — Red Terror — Opposition banned

5. Foundations of the Cold War: Capitalism vs. Communism — 1947 to 1991

6. Communist International (Comintern, 1919): World revolution का organization

7. The Comintern forced Western democracies to appease Germany and Italy—fear of communism

B. Social impact

1. End of class distinction: Aristocratic privileges ended – "All men equal"

2. Land Redistribution: Land to Peasants

3. Women's equality: Equal voting rights, education, employment — women doctors, judges, factory workers, scientists

4. Secular Education: Literacy almost universal by 1939, expansion of secondary and higher education

5. Improvement in workers' conditions: 8-hour workday, social security, free healthcare

6. Civil War 1918-21: 7 million+ deaths — a heavy price

C. Economic impact

1. Land Nationalization: estates of landlords → peasants

2. State-owned industries: Private industry → workers' control → State control

3. Planned Economy: Five Year Plans — Centralized economic planning का model

4. NEP (1921-28): Limited capitalism की वापसी — "Mixed economy"

5. Rapid Industrialization: 1928-41 — USSR = world's 2nd industrial power

D. ideological influence

1. Marxism-Leninism: The practical application of Marxism—adapted by Lenin

2. Europe's ideological divide: Communism vs. Capitalism—the first such divide since the Protestant Reformation

3. Rise of Fascism: Italy (1922), Germany (1933) — Anti-Communist reaction — Industrialists supported the Fascists
